## 🔬 Methodology

### Assessment Framework

This evaluation follows OSFI's five-domain model risk assessment structure:

1️⃣  Risk Identification (E-23 Section 4.1)
→ Model purpose, scope, limitations, risk tiering
2️⃣  Model Development (E-23 Section 4.2)
→ Data quality, feature engineering, architecture, documentation
3️⃣  Model Validation (E-23 Section 4.3)
→ Independent validation, performance testing, backtesting
4️⃣  Monitoring & Use (E-23 Section 4.4)
→ Ongoing monitoring, drift detection, incident management
5️⃣  Data Governance (E-23 Section 5.2)
→ Data quality controls, lineage, privacy, security

**Third-Party Risk Assessment (OSFI B-20 Section 3.4):**
- Operational resilience and business continuity
- Concentration risk and vendor dependency
- Exit strategy and data portability
- Regulatory examination support

**Cross-Border Considerations:**
- US regulatory compliance (CFPB, OCC, Federal Reserve)
- Canadian regulatory adaptation requirements
- Data sovereignty and residency

### Evidence Collection

**Total Sources Reviewed:** 85+ documents

**Source Categories:**
- 📄 SEC Filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, S-1): 18 documents
- 💼 Investor Relations (presentations, earnings calls): 8 sources
- 🌐 Company Documentation (website, whitepapers): 15 sources
- ⚖️  Regulatory & Compliance: 12 documents
- 📚 Academic Research: 6 peer-reviewed papers
- 📰 Industry Analysis & News: 26 articles

**Evidence Quality Tiers:**
- **Tier 1 (Primary):** SEC filings, regulatory documents (legal accountability)
- **Tier 2 (Secondary):** Investor materials, company publications (official but promotional)
- **Tier 3 (Tertiary):** Academic research, news analysis (independent but may lack detail)

### Scoring Methodology

**Scale:** 0-5 with confidence levels

| Score | Interpretation | Evidence Standard |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| 5 | Exceeds regulatory expectations | Multiple high-quality sources, best-in-class practices |
| 4 | Meets all requirements | Strong evidence across multiple sources |
| 3 | Meets minimum requirements | Adequate evidence, some gaps acceptable |
| 2 | Partial gaps, remediation required | Limited evidence, concerning gaps |
| 1 | Significant gaps, major concerns | Weak evidence, multiple critical issues |
| 0 | Critical failure, unacceptable | No evidence or evidence of non-compliance |

**Confidence Levels:**
- 🟢 **HIGH:** Multiple corroborating public sources, detailed evidence
- 🟡 **MEDIUM:** Limited public evidence, reasonable inferences made
- 🔴 **LOW:** Sparse information, significant assumptions required

## 🎓 Academic Context

**Important Note:** This assessment was conducted as an academic capstone project 
to demonstrate vendor risk management methodology. The analysis is based exclusively 
on publicly available information (SEC filings, investor materials, academic research).

**Limitations Acknowledged:**
- ❌ No vendor interviews or presentations
- ❌ No access to confidential validation reports or proprietary documentation
- ❌ No reference client interviews
- ❌ No model demonstrations or testing
- ❌ No contract negotiation or due diligence verification

**Real-World Application:** In an actual vendor procurement assessment, these 
limitations would be addressed through direct vendor engagement, document review, 
reference validation, and contractual verification. All scores include confidence 
levels reflecting information quality and availability.

This project demonstrates understanding of vendor risk assessment methodology, 
regulatory compliance requirements, and professional risk documentation standards.
